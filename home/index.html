About Me

Note..

I first heard the term “hacker” when I was 13. Something stirred inside me — as if I knew from that moment on, this word would define my life. It felt different, powerful, and I started digging deeper. It wasn’t easy, but it was never impossible. With nothing but a 2GB RAM computer, I made it this far. And I know there’s still a long journey ahead.

Now that I’ve reached a certain level, I approach things more seriously and consciously. I imagine some of you reading this might just be starting your own journey. If your dream is to become a hacker, you need to begin by accepting that this path is challenging. At times, you’ll want to quit. Sometimes, hearing others’ success stories will make you feel left behind or not good enough. There will be moments when nothing seems to work. And then there will be moments when you say, “Yes, I’m really doing this.”

Just don’t forget: The best time to start something was 10 years ago. The second-best time is right now..
